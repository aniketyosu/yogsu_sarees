How to Wear a Saree: A Step-by-Step Guide

Step-by-Step Guide to Draping a Saree:

  1. Choose the Right Saree and Accessories: Select a saree that suits your occasion and pair it with a matching blouse, petticoat, and accessories.
  2. Start with the Petticoat: Wear a well-fitted petticoat underneath your saree and tie it securely at your waist.
  3. Tuck the Saree Into the Petticoat: Tuck the plain end of the saree into the petticoat, starting from your right side and wrapping it around the waist.
  4. Create Pleats: Make 5-7 even pleats and tuck them securely into the petticoat at the center of your waist.
  5. Drape the Pallu Over the Shoulder: Bring the pallu over your left shoulder, letting it fall gracefully.
  6. Adjust the Length: Ensure the length of the saree touches the floor but does not drag. Adjust the pallu length accordingly.
  7. Pin the Pallu in Place: Use safety pins to secure the pallu and pleats in place.

Popular Saree Draping Styles:

  • Nivi Style (Traditional Draping Style): The most common draping style with pleats and pallu over the left shoulder.
  • Bengali Style: Pallu goes from the back and over the right shoulder.
  • Maharashtrian Style: Known as Nauvari, this 9-yard style drapes the saree with the pallu as a scarf.
  • Gujarati Style: Pallu goes over the right shoulder and the pleats are tucked at the front.
  • Lehenga Style: The saree is draped like a lehenga with pleats and a pallu over the shoulder.
  • Modern or Pre-Stitched Saree: A pre-stitched saree eliminates the need for pins and pleats.

Tips for Perfecting Your Saree Look:

  • Practice makes perfect. Keep practicing to improve your saree draping skills.
  • Use safety pins to secure your saree in place.
  • Make sure your blouse is well-fitted to enhance the overall look.
  • Accessorize with the right jewelry, shoes, and clutch for the perfect finish.
  • Ensure comfort while wearing your saree for easy movement.

Conclusion:

Wearing a saree is an art that combines tradition, grace, and beauty. With these simple steps and styling tips, you can wear a saree for any occasion—whether it’s a casual outing, a family gathering, or a grand wedding.
