Types of Sarees by Different Categories

1. Based on Fabric

a) Cotton Sarees

Description: Made from natural cotton fibers, lightweight, breathable, and comfortable for daily wear.

Best for: Summers, casual and office wear.

  • Tant (West Bengal) – Stiff texture, simple borders.
  • Chanderi Cotton (Madhya Pradesh) – Lightweight, glossy finish with zari work.
  • Sambalpuri Cotton (Odisha) – Handwoven, intricate motifs with Ikat dyeing technique.

b) Silk Sarees

Description: Luxurious, soft, and smooth sarees woven from silk fibers.

Best for: Weddings, festive occasions, and grand celebrations.

  • Kanjeevaram (Tamil Nadu) – Heavy silk, rich golden zari work, temple patterns.
  • Banarasi (Uttar Pradesh) – Gold and silver brocade, Mughal-inspired designs, heavy fabric.
  • Mysore Silk (Karnataka) – Soft texture, simple golden border.
  • Baluchari (West Bengal) – Silk with mythological motifs woven in resham thread.

2. Based on Weaving Technique

  • Handloom Sarees – Woven manually, intricate craftsmanship. (Examples: Kanjeevaram, Banarasi)
  • Jacquard Weave Sarees – Designed using jacquard loom, complex patterns.
  • Ikat Sarees – Tie-dye technique on threads before weaving.

3. Based on Region

  • North India – Banarasi, Chikankari Sarees.
  • South India – Kanjeevaram, Mysore Silk.
  • East India – Baluchari, Tant.
  • West India – Bandhani, Paithani.

4. Based on Occasion

  • Daily Wear – Cotton, chiffon, georgette sarees.
  • Office Wear – Linen, crepe, handloom sarees.
  • Weddings – Banarasi, Kanjeevaram, heavily embroidered sarees.
  • Parties – Net, sequined, or embroidered sarees.
  • Festivals – Silk, Bandhani, Chanderi sarees.

5. Based on Embellishments and Work

  • Zari Work Sarees – Gold or silver embroidery (Banarasi, Kanjeevaram).
  • Embroidered Sarees – Hand or machine embroidery (Chikankari, Kashmiri).
  • Printed Sarees – Digital or block prints (Kota Doria, Mulmul cotton).
  • Mirror Work Sarees – Gujarati and Rajasthani styles (Bandhani, Kutchi work).

How to Choose the Right Saree?

  • For Summer: Cotton, chiffon, georgette.
  • For Weddings: Banarasi, Kanjeevaram, Paithani.
  • For Office Wear: Linen, crepe, handloom sarees.
  • For Festivals: Silk, Bandhani, Chanderi.
  • For Parties: Net, sequined, embroidered sarees.
